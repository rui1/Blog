Problem description: You are climbing a stair case. It takes n steps to reach to the top. Each time you can either climb 1 or 2 steps.
In how many distinct ways can you climb to the top?.

Since we can only climb 1 or 2 steps each time, there are only two ways we can reach nth stair.

  1. Climb 2 steps from the (n-2)th stair.
  2. Climb 1 steps from the (n-1)th stair.
Let `f(n)` be the number of solutions to reach the `nth` stair. This can be tranlated into a recurrence relation:

$$ f(n)=f(n-1)+f(n-2)$$
$$note: n>2$$

Let's look at some answers for the first few stairs:

steps solution recurrence relation
$$ n= 1$$ $$ 1 $$ $$ - $$
$$ n= 2$$ $$ 2 $$ $$ - $$
$$ n= 3$$ $$ 3 $$ $$ f(1)+f(2)$$
$$ n= 4$$ $$ 5 $$ $$ f(2)+f(3) $$
$$ n= 5$$ $$ 8 $$ $$ f(3)+f(4) $$
$$ n= 6$$ $$ 13 $$ $$ f(4)+f(5) $$
$$ . . .$$ $$ ...$$ $$ ...$$

Examming the first few terms of the sequence, we can recognize that `f(n)` is the Fibonacci sequence.

Reveal Solution
This is typical a dynamic programming question.
  • Time Complexity: `O(n)`
  • Space Complexity: `O(n)`
